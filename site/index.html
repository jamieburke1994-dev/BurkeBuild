Common questions

Straight answers.

Do I need planning permission for an extension?

Not always. In Ireland, many single-storey rear extensions under 40 square metres are exempt from planning permission, subject to conditions - and attic conversions, porches and garage conversions often qualify for exemptions too. We’ll tell you exactly where your project stands, and handle the drawings and application if permission is needed.

How do grants for accessibility works operate?

Local authorities run schemes - including the Housing Adaptation Grant - that can cover a significant share of works like ramps, stairlifts, accessible bathrooms and extensions for a family member with a disability or mobility needs. They are means-tested and must be approved before work starts. We regularly build under these schemes and can guide you through the application.

How long will my project take?

It depends on the job - smaller adaptations are measured in days, extensions in months. What matters is that you’ll have a written programme alongside your quotation before anything starts, so you know the timeline up front.

Do you charge for quotations?

No - quotations are free and without obligation. We visit, measure and talk through the job, then price it in black and white.

Can you help with design?

Yes. We can work from your architect’s drawings or develop the design with you from scratch - and because we build what we draw, we flag buildability and cost issues before they get expensive.

Do we have to move out during a renovation?

Usually not. We phase the work, seal off live areas and keep water and power running where we can. If a stage genuinely needs the house empty, we’ll tell you before we start - not halfway through.
