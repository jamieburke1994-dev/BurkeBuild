Double-Height Kitchen Extension

A kitchen extension that goes up as well as out - exposed brick and steel-framed glazing under a double-height, rooflit ceiling.

The brief

A kitchen worth living in - not just a bigger room, but height, light and texture. The owners wanted drama from the volume and warmth from the materials.

What we did

  • Double-height rear extension with high-level glazing and rooflight
  • Steel-framed glazed doors opening to the courtyard
  • Exposed brick feature wall, cleaned and repointed
  • Herringbone timber flooring throughout
  • Island unit with stone worktop and integrated hob
  • Feature pendant lighting with recessed spots to the perimeter

The result

Daylight falls from two storeys up onto the island, the brick keeps the space warm rather than stark, and the glazing frames the courtyard like a picture. It reads as one considered room, not an add-on.
