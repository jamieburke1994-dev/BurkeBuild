Part M Home Adaptation

A full accessibility adaptation - a level-access concrete ramp with galvanised handrails to the front door, and a fully tiled accessible wet room inside.

The brief

Make the home safe and independent to live in - stepping out the front door and using the bathroom without help, and without the house feeling like a clinic.

What we did

  • Level-access concrete ramp with a slip-resistant finish
  • Galvanised handrails to the ramp and existing steps
  • Accessible wet room with level-access shower
  • Grab rails, fold-down shower seat and accessible sanitaryware
  • Fully tiled walls and slip-resistant flooring
  • All works compliant with Part M of the Building Regulations

Grants

Works like these are often supported by local-authority schemes such as the Housing Adaptation Grant. They're means-tested and need approval before work starts - we regularly build under them and can guide you through the application.
